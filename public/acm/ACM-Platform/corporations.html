ACM for Corporations

ACM is all about using the best of search engine techniques. Search is already integral to our lives, so ACM seeks to apply these techniques in a way that can help us analyse how to pursue that dream job better.

Hard to find the right people for the right job?

Companies usually have their own set of competencies required in their own hiring process. The high numbers of candidates combined with their varied experiences from the CVs and resumes makes it immensely menial to shortlist and rank candidates relevant to the role.

While GPAs and specializations are certainly effective measures of aptitude, the sheer numbers of applicants in entry level roles means that a varied experience is essential in distinguishing candidate’s performance and suitability for the role. For fresh hires, this problem of similar competencies is particularly acute with graduates being cut from a similar mould. This is even more so in mid-career hires where the knowledge of candidates cannot be easily quantified. It is hence important to have a way to effectively measure candidate’s relevancy in an efficient manner

Why ACM?

A feature of ACM is that it allows business to map job roles description into a skills framework (currently only SFIA). Inputting the description of the job roles will return a list of required skills and its respective level. Such an ability could allow enterprise to evaluate both the job roles and current employees skills into a similar framework, helping the shuffling and employing of the right employees to the right job roles.
